To my future self:

My learning process using VIM key bindings was stuck on move commands; even though I realized the power of moving freely and accurately around the editor, I hadn’t reason to waste my life with key bindings. This post is a never-ending excuse to continue developing my VIM workflow. If I am reading this now, I am sure I forgot something.

Repeating a char or a seq of chars n times

Sequence: ESC-n-i-char/seq-ESC-ESC

  • n: number of times
  • i: insert mode
  • char/seq: char or sequence of chars

Usage: Create a header for a file or when I need to repeat an arbitrary sequence of characters.
